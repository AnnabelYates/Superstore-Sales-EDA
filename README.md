# Superstore-Sales-EDA
I used Tableau to identify sales trends for a superstore. My analysis was guided by the book Visual Analytics with Tableau by Alexander Loth. 

## Executive Summary:

## Business Problem:

A superstore needed to identify sales trends using sales data from the last four years. These findings will help the company focus their business goals for the future to maximize sales and profits. 

#### What are sales and profit trends for the business as a whole, regionally, by state/province, and individually at city level?   

## Methodology:

1) Create basic data visualizations using the Superstore Sales dataset from Tableau's sample data. 

2) Utilize aggregate functions, calculated fields and parameters to create more complex data visualizations.

3) Perform table calculations and level of detail calculations to produce visualizations of further complexity.

4) Create maps to explore geographical trends within the dataset.

5) Create an interactive dashboard to present sales and profits trends.

6) Create a Tableau story to summarize this learning experience and the major findings from the exploratory data analysis.

## Results and Business Recommendations:

### Major Findings: 

1) In the most recent quarter (Qtr 3, 2026), the company has made $198,183.79 in sales. The sales forecast for the remainder of 2026 and 2027 predicts sales will mirror those in 2025, staying steady after the strong gains in 2023 and 2024.

2) Recurrent annual trends indicate that holidays are a large driver of sales for the business. The last quarter of every year with the end of year holidays has persistently had the highest annual sales. Monthly sales data shows that November and December are the months with the highest sales by a significant difference. The business has minor spikes in sales in March and September, which suggests Easter and spring holidays as well as back-to-school sales lead to an increase in sales also.

3) Regionally, the west and east stores have the highest overall sales, as well as profit. The central region has a moderate amount of sales and the south region has the lowest sales of all regions by a significant difference.

4) High sales do not always equal high profits for the business. For instance, in the east region sales of furniture were over $210,000 but the profit from this category was only $3,444.74. Further, in the central region furniture sales over $160,0000 produced no profit and caused the company to lose $2,082.21.
